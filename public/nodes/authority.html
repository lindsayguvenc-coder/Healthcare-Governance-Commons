Authority Tiers

What action is this governance body authorized to take?

Known Contention & Gap
⚡ Contention
Tier 3/4 authority is politically difficult to assign — institutions resist hard stop authority
⬜ Known Gap
Hard stop authority is formally unassigned at most institutions. Fallback protocols often do not exist at deployment.
Components
Tier 1: AdvisoryTier 2: ConditionalTier 3: Suspend (requires fallback)Tier 4: Terminate (CMO+CIO authority)
Authority follows accountability. Whoever already owns corrective action in your governance system should own AI governance authority for that domain.

Practitioner Insights
The gap I keep finding: institutions build AI governance committees but don't pre-assign who has Tier 3 (suspend) and Tier 4 (terminate) authority. Everyone agrees those tiers should exist. Nobody wants to put a name on them before something goes wrong. That reluctance is itself a governance failure — and it's the most common one.
